CrashStealer macOS malware uses a notarized dropper to pass Gatekeeper checks

Jamf Threat Labs flagged CrashStealer, a native C++ macOS information stealer distributed through a signed and Apple-notarized Werkbit.app disk image that can pass Gatekeeper checks. It targets browsers, cryptocurrency wallets, password managers and the keychain, so Mac-heavy engineering teams should treat notarization as insufficient trust for developer workstations.

The Hacker News

Forg365 phishing-as-a-service targets Microsoft 365 with device-code and AitM session theft

ZeroBAC described Forg365 as a Telegram-sold PhaaS kit for Microsoft 365 that combines device-code phishing, adversary-in-the-middle tactics, antibot evasion, AI-assisted lure creation, token vaulting and post-compromise mailbox operations. The industrialized workflow means Microsoft 365 defenses need device-code and OAuth controls plus token/session detection, not just credential-phishing training.

The Hacker News

Apple says former employee exploited a rare authentication bug after leaving for OpenAI

Apple's trade-secrets complaint alleges former employee Chang Liu exploited a rare, previously unknown authentication bug to access shared network folders and download confidential files weeks after leaving for OpenAI. Apple says it fixed the bug after discovering the breach; the operational lesson is to verify deprovisioning with logs and anomaly monitoring, not just account-disable workflows.

CISA warns Russia-linked hackers are exploiting home and small-office routers as proxy infrastructure

CISA and international partners warned that Russia FSB Center 16 actors continue exploiting poorly configured and vulnerable home and small-office networking devices to obscure operations against critical infrastructure and other organizations. For distributed teams and small sites, router patching, credential rotation and exposure checks now sit inside the enterprise threat surface.

Ars Technica

Tracebit shows defensive prompt injections can halt AI hacking agents

Tracebit researchers reported that placing defensive prompt injections alongside AWS-stored secrets can push attacking AI agents into guardrail violations that make them shut down, a technique Ars describes as context bombing. It is not a primary control, but it is a useful reminder that model-context behavior can be turned into a tripwire against autonomous recon and secret-harvesting agents.

The takeaway is concrete for enterprise agent teams: the harness, evaluation loop and refusal path matter as much as the base model.

GenCeption repurposes a video diffusion model into a unified vision model

GenCeption from Google DeepMind is described as a feed-forward, text-steered vision model built by repurposing a pretrained video generative model and post-training it across dense and sparse vision tasks. The technical idea worth studying is the collapse of a multi-step video diffusion backbone into a single-step perception model, aiming to move computer vision from task-specific systems toward a generalist model.
