North Korea-linked PolinRider campaign plants 108 malicious packages and extensions

The Hacker News reports that North Korean actors tied to Contagious Interview published 108 packages and browser extensions across npm, Packagist, Go modules, and Chrome, with payloads also triggered through developer tooling such as VS Code task files. The campaign has compromised public GitHub repositories and is delivering DEV#POPPER RAT and OmniStealer variants.

Alibaba reportedly bans Claude Code inside the company

TechCrunch reports that Alibaba will ban employees from using Anthropic's Claude Code from July 10 after classifying it as high-risk software. The report says Anthropic already prohibits Chinese companies and their foreign-owned entities from using its models, while Alibaba is directing staff toward its own Qoder tool.

techcrunch.com

Wafer claims GLM 5.2 inference on AMD MI355X reaches about 80% of B200 throughput

Wafer says its engineers served GLM 5.2 on AMD MI355X at 2,626 tokens per second per node and 213 tokens per second single-stream, claiming about 80% of B200 throughput at more than 2x lower cost. The claim is presented as an AI inference benchmark rather than a general model release.

x.com

Midjourney asks Hollywood studios to disclose more of their own AI usage

In its copyright dispute with Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros., Midjourney is seeking broader discovery into how the studios use generative AI, including internal use for storyboarding or ideation and prompts used in Midjourney. A judge had previously limited disclosure to AI use that produced consumer-facing videos and images.
